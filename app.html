Reference Options

When entering a sale, reps pick one of these to identify it for verification — whatever makes sense for how your office looks things up (a spreadsheet row number, a transaction ID, a date, etc). For sales spanning two records, a single reference lets the verifier pull up both.

Verification Steps

These are the YES/NO checks your verifier runs on each entry before approving it. Add, remove, or rename them to match your industry and workflow. All checks must pass (plus initials) before an entry is marked approved.

Combined Contest Doubling
Off

When on, doubling is based on a rep's total sales across all contest types combined — not per individual type. Once a rep hits the combined threshold, every additional sale in any contest type doubles.

Example: Threshold set to 9. A rep logs 3 PIFs, 3 bundles, 3 retention saves = 9 total. Their 10th sale — in any of those three types — automatically pays double.
Which contests count toward the combined threshold?

Types not selected use their own individual threshold and never contribute to the combined count.

AI Insights Beta
✨ Weekly Summary

A plain-English readout of this week's activity — entries, payouts, and anything that looks worth a second look. Saves you from scanning the Verify queue line by line.

✨ Anomaly Check

Scans recent entries for patterns worth a closer look — unusual volume spikes, repeated reference numbers, or payouts that don't match a rep's normal history. This doesn't auto-deny anything; it just points you at what to check before you approve.

✨ Describe Your Spiff Program

Skip the form. Describe your incentive program in plain English and we'll set up the spiff types, rates, and thresholds for you — review and adjust anything before saving.

AI features are in beta. Suggestions are a starting point — you always review and approve before anything is saved or paid out.
